# Content marketing basics

Plan and ship content that attracts and converts without random posting.

## Strategy

- Define audience, pain points, and one measurable goal (leads, trials, newsletter).
- Content pillars: 3–5 themes you own (e.g. tutorials, comparisons, case studies).
- Map topics to funnel stage: awareness, consideration, decision.

## Formats

- **Blog/guides:** depth, SEO, evergreen updates.
- **Landing pages:** single offer, one CTA, social proof above fold.
- **Email:** sequences tied to behavior (signup, trial day 3, churn risk).
- **Video/shorts:** hook in 3 seconds; link to a owned page with CTA.

## Production workflow

- Brief: audience, promise, outline, primary keyword (if search matters).
- Draft → edit for clarity → add visuals → publish → distribute once, repurpose twice.
- Maintain a simple calendar; batch creation beats daily scramble.

## Distribution

- Owned channels first (site, email list); then social where audience actually is.
- Repurpose: long article → thread, carousel, newsletter snippet.
- Track UTM links per campaign; compare channels by conversion, not likes.

## Quality bar

- Specific headlines beat vague ("How we cut deploy time 40% with feature flags").
- Show outcomes, screenshots, or numbers when possible.
- Update top performers yearly; redirect or merge thin duplicate posts.
